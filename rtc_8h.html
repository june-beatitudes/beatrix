struct  bea_datetime
 Represents a single point in time in the Gregorian calendar. Years begin at 2000 (since the RTC hardware can't represent anything earlier than that), and hours are in 24-hour time. Always in UTC because the IANA timezone database is in itself bigger than the amount of flash we have to work with. More...

◆ bea_datetime_to_kstrz()

void bea_datetime_to_kstrz ( const struct bea_datetime datetime,
char * buf )

Fills a buffer of at least 31 chars (please just use BEA_DATETIME_KSTRZ_LEN) with a null terminated string in BEATRIX kernel format.

The BEATRIX kernel format is loosely based on ISO and is "WDY MON DY YEAR HR:MN:SECOND UTC", with seconds being given with 5 significant figures of precision and a decimal point.

Parameters
datetimeThe datetime to use
bufThe buffer to fill
